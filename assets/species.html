All 23 Types of Foxes

There are 23 known species of foxes. The most popular is the red fox.

Genus Vulpes

Includes all the animals listed as true foxes:

  • Bengal Fox / Indian Fox (Vulpes bengalensis): The smallest of the true foxes. The population is decreasing, but it is listed as of least concern.
  • Blanford’s Fox (Vulpes cana): Small fox found in the Middle East and southwestern Asia.
  • Cape Fox (Vulpes chama): Lives in southern Africa. Heavily hunted but not endangered.
  • Corsac Fox (Vulpes corsac): Found in central Asia. Hunted for fur.
  • Tibetan Sand Fox (Vulpes ferrilata): High-altitude species. Very little is known.
  • Arctic Fox (Vulpes lagopus): Critically Endangered. White and blue color morphs. Threatened by the fur trade.
  • Kit Fox (Vulpes macrotis): Southwestern U.S. predator, related to the Swift Fox.
  • Pale Fox (Vulpes pallida): Camouflaged desert dweller with five subspecies.
  • Rüppell’s Fox (Vulpes rueppellii): North African fox expanding due to desertification.
  • Swift Fox (Vulpes velox): Nearly extinct in the 1930s, now recovered in the U.S.
  • Red Fox (Vulpes vulpes): Global distribution. At least 45 subspecies. Hunted for fur and sport.
  • Fennec Fox (Vulpes zerda): Sahara species with large ears adapted for heat regulation.
