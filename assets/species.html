All 23 Types of Foxes

There are 23 foxes. The most popular is the red fox.

Genus Vulpes

Includes all the animals listed as true foxes:

  • Bengal Fox/Indian Fox(Vulpes bengalensis)

    * Is the smallest of the true foxes. Population is decreasing but is currently listed as a species of least concern for conservation

  • Blanford’s Fox(Vulpes cana)

    * This is a small canine that resides in parts of Middle East and southwestern Asia.

  • Cape Fox(Vulpes chama)

    * Resides throughout southern Africa. They are heavily hunted but are listed as species of least concern.

  • Corsac Fox(Vulpes corsac)

    * Resides throughout central Asia. They are hunted for their fur.

  • Tibetan Sand Fox(Vulpes ferrilata)

    * Resides high upon the Tibetan Plateau. Very little is known about the species. They live far from major areas of human development.

  • Arctic Fox(Vulpes lagopus)

    Critically Endangered
    * One of the most identifiable fox species. They have two colour morphs: the white morph(commonly seen) and the blue morph. Categorized as Critically Endangered (caused by the fur trade).

  • Kit Fox(Vulpes macrotis)

    * Resides throughout the United States and northern Mexico. They primarily eat Merriam's kangaroo rats and black-tailed jackrabbits. They are closely related to the Swift Fox.

  • Pale Fox(Vulpes pallida)

    * One of the least-studied canines in the world. Resides in the remote part of the African Sabel. The pale fure helps them blend into their envirnoments. There is at least five subspecies.

  • Rüppell’s Fox(Vulpes rueppellii)

    * Has a wide geographic distribution. Natural range is growing due to rapid desertification. They primarily live in North Africa.

  • Swift Fox(Vulpes velox)

    * Resides in the western Great Plains and southern Rocky Mountains. In the 1930s it was almost driven to extinction. However, it is no longer endangered in the United States.

  • Red Fox(Vulpes vulpes)

    * Can be found everywhere. There's at least 45 subspecies. It is one of the most persecuted canines in the world. They are treated as pests, used for its soft fur, and used as game in hunting for sport.

  • Fennec Fox(Vulpes zerda)

    * Resides in the Sahara Desert and arid parts of Israel. It's known for its huge ears. These ears are a special adaptation that helps them dissipate their body heat and cool off. They also give these foxes keen hearing.

Genus Urocyon

Not "true foxes" but are closely related to Genus Vulpes:

  • Gray Fox(Urocyon cinereoargenteus)

    Near-threatened
    * Resides throughout North and Central America. One of the most highly-skilled tree-climbing foxes. At least 16 subspecies. Some are regionally endangered or threatened.

  • Island Fox(Urocyon littoralis)

    Near-threatened
    * Lives only on six out of the eight Channel Islands off the coast of southern California. There's 6 subspecies. Each subspecies lives on a different island. At least four are considered endangered. As a whole the island fox is near endangered.

Genus Otocyon

A basal species:

  • Bat-Eared Fox(Otocyon megalotis)

    * Two recognized subspecies. Resides on the African savanna. Its ears provide a streamlined way to dissipate body heat and cool off like the Fennec Fox.

Genus Lycalopex

South America Foxes. Six living species:

  • Culpeo Fox/Andean Fox(Lycalopex culpaeus)

    * Second largest canine in South America. Stable Population. Biggest threats are human hunting and introduced diseases.

  • Darwin's Fox(Lycalopex fulvipes)

    Endangered
    * Endangered Species. Resides in isolated areas of Chile. Discovered by Charles Darwin in 1834. Few thousand left in the wild.

  • South American Gray Fox(Lycalopex griseus)

    * Also known as the chilla or the Patagonian fox. Resides in the southernmost part of South America. Hunted for fur. It can weigh up to 12lbs(5.5kg).

  • Pampas Fox/Azara's Fox(Lycalopex gymnocercus)

    * Resides throughout the Pampas region of South America. Five recognized subspecies. Hunted for fur and by farmers protecting their livestock. Habitat loss.

  • Sechuran Fox/Peruvian Desert Fox(Lycalopex sechurae)

    Near-threatened
    * Among rarest canines in northern South America. Is nocturnal. Listed as near-threatened. Hunted for use in folk medicine.

  • Hoary Fox(Lycalopex vetulus)

    Near-threatened
    * Near-threatened species. Only found in central and western Brazil. Agile hunter. Nocturnal. Generally eats insects, but given the opportunity also eats small rodents.

Genus Cerdocyon

Closely related to members of the genus Lycalopex and the various wolf species:

  • Crab-Eating Fox(Cerdocyon thous)

    * AKA Bush dog, Maikong, and wood fox. Doglike canine. Skilled hunters. Threatened by invasive species and introduced diseases.

Genus Canis

Debated whether this species is a fox. Part of the genus that includes coyotes, golden jackals, domestic dogs, and different species of wolves:

  • Simien Fox(Canis simensis)

    Critically Endangered
    * AKA as Ethiopian Wolf or Simien Jackal. One of the most highly endangered throughout its range. Habitat loss and introduced diseases.
